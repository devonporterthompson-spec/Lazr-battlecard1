Handling objections
"Your competitor is cheaper."
Rate on the label is one number. The real number is what you actually pay after adjustments, re-class fees, and claims disputes. FreightCOM and ShipTime both have documented cases of shippers being charged 3–5× the quoted price weeks later — no warning, no transparency. Lazr automates invoice auditing so what you quote is what clears. And we're free to start, so the risk of trying us is literally zero.
"We need Shopify / eCommerce integrations."
Fair — that's on our roadmap and I won't pretend otherwise. But ask yourself: what share of your volume is eCommerce parcel vs. freight and pallets? ShipStation and ClickShip are great for boxes — the moment you're shipping pallets, they're done. If you're doing any LTL, FTL, or cross-border freight at all, Lazr handles the full operation. If Shopify is 100% of your business, let's talk timeline — but most B2B shippers have mixed volume and freight needs.
"We already use a freight forwarder we trust."
That relationship doesn't go away — you can load their rates right into Lazr's platform. What Lazr adds is the visibility layer your forwarder can't give you: real-time tracking, consolidated billing across all carriers, KPI dashboards your whole team can see, and automated invoice auditing. You're not replacing the relationship — you're getting the technology layer on top of it.
"We don't have time to learn a new platform."
One of our customers with 6 locations said the exact same thing. After switching, their whole team could track everything without calling the freight department. Onboarding is fast, free to start, and our trilingual support team is hands-on through setup. The time cost of learning Lazr is a fraction of the time your team spends today chasing invoices and jumping between carrier portals.
"We already have a TMS — Kuebix / FreightPOP / Freightview."
Those are solid platforms for specific use cases. Freightview stops at LTL — no parcel, no Canadian carriers, no ERP integration (their own users are asking for it). Kuebix is built for enterprise teams with enterprise complexity. FreightPOP has no mobile access and a steep setup curve. If you're leaving money on the table in any of those gaps, Lazr fills them — and you can layer it in without replacing what you have.
"The numbers just don't work for us right now."
Rates matter — full stop. What I'd want to get clear on is the full cost picture: label rate + time on billing disputes + re-class fees from NMFC errors + unresolved claims. Lazr is free to start, so there's no financial risk in running us alongside what you have for 30 days. If we can't demonstrate savings on real volume, that's a real answer — not just a number on a slide.
"We're happy with what we have."
Great — and I'm not here to create a problem that doesn't exist. What I would ask is: when did you last benchmark your carrier rates? Do you have full visibility across all locations today? Are your invoices audited automatically? Most of our customers thought they were fine until they saw how much was leaking in billing gaps and manual time. Happy to run a no-commitment rate comparison on a few recent shipments — the data makes the case better than I can.
Feature ideas — what would close more deals
🔴 High priority
Shopify + WooCommerce + BigCommerce
The #1 objection you're losing on. FreightCOM/ClickShip and ShipTime both have this. Even a lightweight parcel-rate widget at checkout would remove this blocker entirely for eCommerce prospects.
🔴 High priority
Automated claims management portal
The biggest pain point across ALL competitors. FreightCOM, ShipTime, and Freightview all get hammered on claims handling. A structured portal where clients file, track, and resolve claims inside Lazr would be a direct rebuttal to "what if something goes wrong."
🟡 Medium priority
Branded tracking page for shippers
ShipStation's biggest loyalty driver. Lets your clients offer their customers a white-labeled tracking experience — turns Lazr into a customer-facing tool, not just an ops tool. Dramatically increases stickiness.
🟡 Medium priority
Mobile app (iOS + Android)
FreightPOP and Freightview have no mobile — a real gap. Warehouse managers and drivers on the floor need mobile tracking. Even a lightweight app for tracking + booking confirmation closes this objection.
🟡 Medium priority
Spend analytics dashboard
Executives want freight spend by lane, carrier, location, and period. A visual dashboard that finance and ops leadership use makes Lazr a boardroom tool, not just a booking tool — much harder to rip out once embedded at that level.
🟡 Medium priority
Vendor / supplier inbound portal
Let clients' suppliers book inbound shipments directly via a Lazr-branded link. The move from "tool your team uses" to "tool your whole supply chain uses" — massive stickiness. Freightview offers a basic version and it's a top-rated feature.
🔵 Nice to have
CO₂ / carbon emissions reporting
Growing procurement requirement for enterprise and publicly traded companies in QC/ON. Freightera markets this. It's a checkbox that opens RFP doors that would otherwise be closed before the conversation starts.
🔵 Nice to have
Rate benchmarking vs. market
Show prospects what they're overpaying vs. market rates — a built-in rate audit tool. This converts the "happy with what we have" objection into a live demo closer. The data makes the pitch for you before you even have to say a word.
